CHIN CHIN

Description

Chin chin is a fried Nigerian snack. It is known as atchomon in Togo and Benin, achomo in Ghana and croquette in Cameroon. It is similar to Scandinavian snack klenat, a chrunchy, donut-like baked or fried dough of wheat flour. Chin-chin may contain cowpeas but the major ingredient is flour.

Ingredient

  • 3 cups flour [375 grams]
  • 1\2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • 1\2 cup of sugar [100 grams]
  • 1\2 baking margarine [115 grams]
  • 1\2 to 2\3 cup of liquid milk or water
  • 1 egg [optional. Use if you want a soft chin chin
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Combine flour, baking powder and sugar in a bowl. Slice in margarine and mix until well incorporated [It should look like breadcrumbs]
  2. Crack in egg mix
  3. Gently add water into flour mix until you have a slighty stretchy but not sticky dough [do not add too much water at once]
  4. Roll out the dough on a flat surface and cut into desired shapes
  5. Set the oil on medium heat, wait until the oil is hot. Fry the shaped dough a batch at a time until golden brown

    6. Enjoy!
